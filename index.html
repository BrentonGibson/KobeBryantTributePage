Kobe Bean Bryant

Kobe Bean Bryant was an American professional basketball player who was born on August 23, 1978, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, and died tragically on January 26, 2020, in a helicopter crash.

About the Legend

☛ Kobe spent his entire 20-year NBA career with the Los Angeles Lakers.

☛ A five-time NBA champion (2000, 2001, 2002, 2009, 2010).

☛ Named NBA Finals MVP twice (2009, 2010).

☛ He was the youngest player in NBA history to reach 30,000 career points.

☛ Selected to 18 NBA All-Star Games, and recieved 4 NBA All-Star Game MVPs (2002, 2007, 2009, 2011).

☛ NBA Rookie of the Year in 1997.

☛ Known for his scoring ability, footwork, and competitive spirit.

☛ NBA scoring champion multiple times.

☛ Two-time Olympic gold medalist, representing the United States.

☛ 2nd on the NBA's all-time career scoring list at the time of his retirement.

☛ Named to the All-NBA First Team multiple times, and named to the NBA All-Defensive First Team multiple times.

☛ Kobe scored 81 points in a single game against the Toronto Raptors in 2006, the second-highest point total in a single game in NBA history.

☛ Beyond basketball, he was involved in various philanthropic efforts and creative projects.

☛ Kobe was often compared to basketball legends like Michael Jordan.

☛ His work ethic "Mamba Mentality" inspired athletes and individuals worldwide.

☛ Known for his charitable work, he established Kobe and Vanessa Bryant Family Foundation.

☛ An accomplished writer and producer, Kobe won an Academy Award for his animated short film, "Dear Basketball.".
